## ✍️ The post (paste body — ~1,900 chars)

```
I stood in a grocery aisle holding two cans of the same beans, googling which company was the "good" one.

Forty minutes later I had no answer. Just opinions.

One blog said boycott them. One said they'd reformed. A Reddit thread argued about something from 2014. An "ethical shopping" app gave a glowing score — which turned out to be the company's own self-reported ESG report.

Everyone had a take. Nobody had a receipt.

That bugged me, because the facts existed. They were just buried.

Companies file with the FEC. The EPA logs their violations. OSHA cites their safety records. The SEC and DOJ publish enforcement actions. It's all public — just scattered across 200+ databases nobody has time to read.

So I spent the last year reading them. Nights, weekends, a lot of coffee.

The result is TruNorth. Scan a barcode or search a brand, and you get a letter grade. But the grade isn't the point.

Tap it, and you land on the actual document — an FEC filing, an EPA penalty on the federal record, an OSHA citation with a date. You're not trusting me. You're reading the filing.

Most ethical-shopping apps run on scraped reviews or a company's own ESG brochure — marketing, laundered into a score. I wanted the opposite. Records, not opinions.

A 30-second quiz tunes the grade to what you care about. I'm not here to tell you what to value — I'm here to show you the record. You decide.

Honest about where it stands:
• 12,000+ brands tracked
• ~2,900 fully graded so far
• the rest show a "?" until the data exists

I'd rather show you a question mark than a number I can't back with a source.

No streaks. No push spam. No outrage feed. A tool you open when you have a question, then close.

It launched 5 days ago. It's just me. It's the tool I wish I'd had in that aisle.

If you've ever stood there googling — this one's for you. Scan a few things in your pantry and tell me which grade surprised you most; that feedback is what shapes the next ones.

Link in the first comment 👇

#buildinpublic #indiehackers #iOSdev #consciousconsumer
```
