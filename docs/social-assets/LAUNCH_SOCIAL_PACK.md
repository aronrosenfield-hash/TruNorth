## 📸 Instagram

**Caption:**
```
I stood in a grocery aisle holding two cans of beans, googling which company was the "good" one. 40 minutes later: no answer. Just opinions. 🫘

One blog said boycott. One said they'd reformed. A Reddit thread was still arguing about something from 2014. An "ethical shopping" app gave a glowing score that turned out to be the company's own report about itself.

Everyone had a take. Nobody had a receipt.

Here's the thing: the facts actually exist. Companies file with the FEC. The EPA logs violations. OSHA cites safety records. The SEC and DOJ publish enforcement actions. It's all public — just scattered across 200+ databases nobody has time to read.

So I spent a year reading them.

That became TruNorth. Scan a barcode or search a brand → you get a letter grade → tap it and land on the actual document. An FEC filing. An EPA penalty. An OSHA citation with a date on it. Records, not vibes.

A 30-second quiz tunes the grade to what YOU care about across 9 categories. I'm not here to tell you what to value. I'm here to show you the record. You decide.

Being honest: 12,000+ brands tracked, ~2,900 fully graded so far, the rest show a "?". No streaks, no push spam, no outrage feed to scroll. Just receipts.

Launched 5 days ago. Built it solo. 🧭

Free on iPhone — link in bio.

What's the one brand you've always wondered the truth about? 👇
```

## 👍 Facebook

**Post (link works in-post):**
```
A few months ago I stood in a grocery aisle holding two cans of beans, trying to figure out which company was the "good" one.

Forty minutes of googling later, I had nothing. One blog said boycott them. Another said they'd cleaned up their act. A Reddit thread was still arguing about something from 2014. An "ethical shopping" app gave one of them a glowing score — which turned out to be the company grading its own homework from its own ESG report.

Everyone had an opinion. Nobody had a receipt.

And the strange part is the actual facts DO exist. Companies file political donations with the FEC. The EPA logs pollution violations. OSHA records safety citations. The SEC and DOJ publish enforcement actions. It's all public — it's just buried across 200+ separate government databases that no normal person is going to sit and read.

So I did. I spent about a year reading them, and I built the thing I wanted in that aisle.

It's called TruNorth. You scan a barcode or search a brand, you get a letter grade — and here's the part I care about most: you can tap the grade and land on the actual document. The real FEC filing. The EPA penalty. The OSHA citation with a date on it. Records, not vibes.

A quick 30-second quiz tunes the grade to what matters to YOU, across 9 different categories. I'm genuinely not here to tell anyone what to care about. I just want to put the receipts on the table and let you decide.

And I want to be honest about where it stands: there are 12,000+ brands tracked, but only about 2,900 are fully graded so far — the rest still show a "?" until I get them there. No streaks, no notifications nagging you, no outrage feed. Just the record.

I launched it five days ago. It's just me — solo-built. It's free, with an optional Pro tier if you want to support it.

If you've ever stood in that same aisle wondering, I'd love for you to try it and tell me what you think. What's the first brand you'd look up?

Download (iPhone): https://apps.apple.com/app/id6775301458

#ConsciousConsumer #PublicRecords
```

- **Media:** Lead with the 9-slide carousel — Facebook renders carousels as a swipeable gallery that rewards the story-driven scroll, and the first slide should be the "two cans of beans in the aisle" hook so the image matches the narrative opener. If forced to a single image, use the verdict/grade screen (letter grade + tap-through to a real document), since that's the one frame that makes "records, not opinions" instantly legible. The 30s captioned video is a strong alternate primary — FB autoplays muted, and the burned-in captions/VO let the demo land without sound; it would also be the best thing to drop as a follow-up in the comments to re-surface the post.
- **Link:** In-post, near the end, right before the engagement question's close — on Facebook in-post links are fully functional and don't get throttled the way they do on IG/X, so no need to bury it in a comment. Keep the raw App Store URL (https://apps.apple.com/app/id6775301458) on its own line so FB generates a clean link preview. Optionally repeat it in the first comment for anyone who skims.
- **Tips:** Post timing: weekday morning or early evening CST (Aron's tz) catches FB's broader/older audience during commute/wind-down scroll windows. Keep hashtags to 2 — FB isn't hashtag-driven and more reads as spammy. The opening 1-2 lines are everything: FB truncates with a "See more" fold around ~250 chars, so the beans-in-the-aisle hook has to do the work before the cut. Reply to EVERY early comment within the first hour — FB's algorithm weights comment velocity + author replies heavily, and the closing question ("What's the first brand you'd look up?") is designed to farm exactly that. Expect people to name brands in comments; have 3-4 genuinely surprising graded examples ready to reply with (with the tap-through receipt) to keep the thread alive. Avoid any "vote with your wallet" framing in replies too — stay neutral/journalistic. If reach stalls, drop the 30s video as a comment a few hours in to re-surface the post in feeds.
