# v1.2 Grading Redesign — analysis & prototype (2026-06-29)

> Working design doc for the "start-at-A" grading model + personalization, with three
> data-grounded simulations run against the live catalog (12,839 tracked / 2,840 graded).
> Prototypes live in scratchpad (`drift_sim.mjs`, `start_at_a.mjs`, `persona_sim.mjs`).
> **Nothing here is shipped** — this is the v1.2 design basis. Current scoring (R7.1,
> shrink-to-50) is unchanged in Build 76.

## The problem
The current model anchors every gradeable company at **50 (neutral ≈ B-/C+)** and
shrinks toward it. Because the public record is overwhelmingly a record of *harm* and
credible *positives* are scarce, grades skew low and pile into a **mushy B/C middle**
(live distribution among graded: A 3% · B 36% · C 36% · D 17% · F 8%). Adding more
sources doesn't fix this — see the drift analysis — because the new records are mostly
display-only, negative/compliance, or stance-gated.

dims (supplyChain, fedReserve, secLitigation) — i.e., they punish transparency and size.

### Tax principle (Aron, 2026-06-29)
Paying a low *legal* tax rate is not misconduct. Tax stays a **transparency datapoint on
the card, never a demerit.** Same logic applies to other legal-but-informational records
(conflict-minerals filings, lobbying disclosures): show them, don't grade them down.

## 2. The start-at-A model

**Spec:** start at 100 → deduct only for genuinely-bad categories (`score < 48`); the
**worst issue counts full, additional severe issues compound** (×0.6, ×0.4, ×0.25…),
minor records fade; small credit for verified-good categories; **coverage gate** sends
thin-data brands to "?"; **relabel A as "Clean record."** Tax excluded.
Thresholds (recalibrated for the new scale): **A≥88 · B≥70 · C≥52 · D≥36 · F<36.**

**Distribution (among the 2,840 gradeable):**

| Grade | Current (shrink-50) | **Start-at-A** |
|---|---|---|
| A | 77 (3%) | **1,000 (35%)** |
| B | 1,018 (36%) | 504 (18%) |
| C | 1,018 (36%) | 922 (32%) |
| D | 492 (17%) | 373 (13%) |
| F | 235 (8%) | **41 (1%)** |

**1,975 move up · 213 move down (real documented harm).** Clean records read as A
(presumption of innocence); F is reserved for *multiple severe* harms; bad actors still
drop (Adobe→D, Alcoa→D, Nike sub-brands→D/F).

**Key clarification:** start-at-A changes the *shape*, not the *coverage*. "?" stays at
9,999 — same brands graded, more fairly. Fewer "?" comes from **more sources** (the
139-source research sweep), a separate lever.

## 3. Personalization — same model, real-human quiz profiles

Six profiles (category weights + stances) applied under start-at-A. Grade per brand×persona:

| Brand | live | Base | Climate | Faith | Labor | Privacy | Animal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Patagonia | A | A | A | **C** | A | A | A |
| Nike | C | D | C | D | **F** | **F** | D |
| ExxonMobil | B | A | **C** | A | A | A | A |
| Walmart | C | D | **F** | C | F | D | F |
| **Chick-fil-A** | C | B | **F** | **B** | D | B | B |
| Ben & Jerry's | A | A | A | **C** | A | A | A |
| Apple | A | B | C | B | **D** | B | B |
| Costco | B | A | A | A | B | A | A |
| Dick's | D | D | D | D | **F** | D | D |
| Amazon | C | D | **F** | D | F | F | F |

**Findings:**
- **Personalization produces real divergence** — brands swing up to 3 letter grades by
  profile. **Chick-fil-A: F for a climate progressive, B for a faith conservative** (the
  political axis, correctly excluded from baseline and only active when a user opts in).
- **Political lean drives the biggest swings** (Chick-fil-A, Patagonia, Ben & Jerry's,
  Exxon) — exactly what a values app should do; it personalizes, it doesn't preach.
- **Issue-weighting sharpens** — the Labor profile pushes Nike/Apple/Amazon down (supply
  chain ×2.5); the Animal profile tanks Amazon (animal-negative); Privacy drops Nike/Amazon.
- **Consistent actors stay put** — Costco A/B everywhere; Amazon D/F everywhere.
  Personalization mostly reshuffles the contested middle.

### Conservative + anti-DEI profile (neutral elsewhere)
DEI is a **directional** axis — the app supports "avoid DEI" symmetrically to "value DEI."

| Brand | Grade | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| **Chick-fil-A** | **B** | right-leaning + DEI-neutral → no stance penalty |
| ExxonMobil | C | right (no political hit) **but pro-DEI** → −DEI |
| Ben & Jerry's | C | left → −political; DEI-neutral |
| Costco | C | pro-DEI → −DEI |
| Apple | D | pro-DEI + labor |
| **Patagonia** | **D** | left **and** pro-DEI (double penalty) |
| Walmart · Amazon · Nike | F | pro-DEI + own record |

**Insight:** because corporate DEI is near-universal, the anti-DEI user finds few high
grades — **Chick-fil-A stands out as the exception** (right + DEI-neutral). Patagonia is the
mirror image of the progressive's favorite (left + pro-DEI = D). The model stays neutral —
same records, weighted by *their* values. DEI weight (×1.5 here) is tunable; a *mild*
preference floats most C/D brands back toward B.

### ⚠️ The data-gap caveat (important)
**ExxonMobil scores A at baseline** — because its environmental record isn't in its
*scored* categories (csc = charity/labor/execPay only). Start-at-A's presumption of
innocence is **only as good as the data**: a missing record reads as "clean." This is the
opacity risk in miniature, and the reason the **coverage gate** (require ≥N categories)
and **more sources** are prerequisites, not nice-to-haves, for shipping start-at-A.

## Recommendations / open knobs
1. **Ship order:** broaden sources (research sweep) + re-derive category severity → *then*
   flip to start-at-A. Don't flip on today's sparse, harm-only data (Exxon=A proves why).
2. **Coverage gate:** ≥1 matches today's set; **≥2** sends ~1,100 thin brands to "?"
   (recommended — no grade off a single signal, closes the Exxon gap partially).
3. **Relabel A** as "Clean record" (not "Excellent") — at 35% it overclaims otherwise.
4. **Tax + legal-disclosure records:** display-only, permanently.
5. **Tunable:** BENCH (harm threshold), compounding decay, thresholds, per-stance weights.
6. Re-derive category scores in the redesign (don't reuse current csc — some drops look harsh).
